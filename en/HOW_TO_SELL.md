# How to Sell Instructions

## Purpose
Train distributors and sales reps to effectively position and sell this product. Reinforce the unique selling points and messaging from the website, sell sheets, and technical specifications.

## Target Audience
- Sales representatives and distributors
- Retail staff selling to contractors
- Anyone responsible for recommending this product to customers

## Content Structure

### 1. **Safety Grabber (15 seconds)**
- Professional tone about product handling
- Builds credibility
- Example: "This is a professional-grade coating. You'll want to know how to talk about its requirements."

### 2. **Opening Hook (30-45 seconds)**
- Attention-grabbing customer benefit
- Why customers should care
- Example: "Your customers want finishes that look great and hold up. This delivers both."

### 3. **Unique Selling Points (2-2.5 minutes)**
- 3-4 key advantages vs. competitors
- Based on website, sell sheets, PDS
- Tied to customer problems they solve
- Examples:
  - "Fast drying means customers can finish their projects faster"
  - "Waterborne means low odor - customers appreciate being in the space"
  - "Won't yellow - keeps that fresh look longer"

### 4. **Application & Customer Fit (1-1.5 minutes)**
- Where and how customers typically use this
- Industry-specific applications
- Types of projects where it shines
- Example: "Perfect for fine furniture and high-end cabinet finishes"

### 5. **Answering Customer Questions (1-1.5 minutes)**
- 2-3 common customer objections
- Clear, confident answers based on data
- Support from PDS or sell sheet
- Examples:
  - "Q: How long does it dry? A: [specific time from PDS]"
  - "Q: Is it durable? A: Yes, it meets these standards..."

### 6. **Safety Reminder & Call to Action (15-30 seconds)**
- Reference the importance of discussing safety with customers
- Direct to SDS for complete information
- Confident closing statement
- Example: "Your customers will appreciate knowing this product requires proper handling. The SDS has all the details you need."

## Key Guidelines
- **Be confident**: You're the expert
- **Use data**: Reference sell sheets and specifications
- **Focus on customer benefits**: "Saves your client money" not "Has low VOC"
- **Address real sales concerns**: What do customers actually ask?
- **Tell success stories**: Real examples are powerful

## Sources
- **Marketing website**: Check for unique selling points and differentiators
- **Sell sheet**: Product benefits, application, competitive advantages
- **PDS (Product Data Sheet)**: Technical specifications, application instructions
- **Sales team feedback**: Common questions and objections

## Example Opening
"You're about to learn how to sell ARROYO Waterborne Clear Topcoat. In the next few minutes, you'll know exactly why your customers should choose this product and how to confidently recommend it. Let's start with what sets it apart."
