# Product Overview Instructions

## Purpose
Provide detailed product knowledge for distributors and sales staff who need to understand technical specifications. All information sourced from the PDS (Product Data Sheet) found on the M.L. Campbell product website.

## Target Audience
- Sales staff needing detailed technical knowledge
- Distributors answering technical customer questions
- Anyone recommending this product to professionals

## Content Structure

### 1. **Safety Grabber (15 seconds)**
- Sets tone for technical responsibility
- Example: "Technical knowledge means better customer recommendations."

### 2. **Product Identification (45 seconds)**
- Official product name
- Product number/code
- Available sizes
- Where it fits in the product line
- Source: PDS cover

### 3. **What It Is & What It Does (1 minute)**
- Type of product (waterborne topcoat, primer, etc.)
- Primary purpose explained simply
- Key composition (waterborne, solvent-based, etc.)
- Source: PDS product description

### 4. **Recommended Uses (45-60 seconds)**
- What it's designed for
- What surfaces it goes on
- Best conditions for application
- Source: PDS "Recommended Uses"

### 5. **Application Basics (1-1.5 minutes)**
- Surface prep needed
- How to apply it (brush, spray, etc.)
- Coverage rates
- Drying times
- Source: PDS "Application Instructions"

### 6. **Performance Data (45-60 seconds)**
- Key performance specifications from PDS
- Drying times
- Appearance and finish
- Coverage information
- Source: PDS "Technical Data"

### 7. **Safety & Special Requirements (45 seconds)**
- Ventilation needs
- PPE requirements
- Temperature/humidity considerations
- Source: PDS and SDS

### 8. **Safety Reminder & Next Steps (15-30 seconds)**
- Reinforce importance of SDS
- Direct customers to safety training
- Example: "For complete safety details, always review the SDS with your customers."

## Key Guidelines
- **Accuracy is critical**: All information from PDS only
- **Speak to professionals**: Use technical terms with this audience
- **Explain the "why"**: Help sales staff understand context
- **Real-world focus**: How does this help them help their customers?
- **Maximum 5 minutes**: If longer, split into separate segments
- **Source every claim**: Cite PDS section for every fact

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ✗ Contradicting the PDS
- ✗ Using outdated technical information
- ✗ Skipping important preparation steps
- ✗ Oversimplifying technical details
- ✗ Ignoring environmental factors in application

## Sources
1. **PDS (Product Data Sheet)** - Your primary source
   - Located on www.mlcampbell.com for each product
   - Download from product page
   - Contains all technical specifications

2. **Safety Data Sheet (SDS)** - For safety-related details only
   - Don't duplicate in this module
   - Reference Safety Knowledge module for detailed safety info

3. **Environmental Data Sheet (EDS)** - For environmental details only
   - Don't duplicate in this module
   - Reference Environmental Knowledge module for detailed environmental info

## Example Opening
"Here's what you need to know to confidently discuss ARROYO Waterborne Clear Topcoat with customers. The next five minutes covers the essentials from the Product Data Sheet. Let's start with the specs that matter most."
