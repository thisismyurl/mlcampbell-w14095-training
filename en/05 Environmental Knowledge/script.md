## Slide 2: VOC Emissions Explained

### Narrative
VOC stands for volatile organic compounds—chemicals that evaporate at room temperature and contribute to air pollution.

Traditional solvent-based finishes contain high levels of VOCs. A typical lacquer might contain 500+ grams of VOCs per liter. These compounds evaporate during application and drying, entering the air we breathe.

ARROYO® 1K contains less than 100 grams of VOCs per liter—a dramatic reduction. By switching to ARROYO® 1K, a shop can reduce VOC emissions by 80 percent or more.

Lower VOC emissions mean:
- Cleaner air in and around your facility
- Reduced worker exposure to harmful fumes
- Lower environmental impact
- Compliance with modern air quality standards

## Slide 3: Environmental Regulations and Compliance

### Narrative
Waterborne topcoats like ARROYO® 1K exist largely because of environmental regulations designed to reduce air pollution.

The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) sets limits on VOC emissions from architectural coatings and wood finishes. These limits have become progressively stricter over the years, driving innovation toward lower-VOC products.

Many states and local jurisdictions have even stricter requirements than federal standards. California, for example, has particularly stringent VOC limits that have driven development of advanced waterborne technologies.

ARROYO® 1K exceeds EPA compliance and meets stricter state and local standards throughout North America. By using ARROYO® 1K, you ensure compliance with current and anticipated future regulations.

## Slide 4: Waterborne Technology Benefits

### Narrative
Waterborne products like ARROYO® 1K offer environmental advantages beyond just lower VOCs.

**Water as Carrier:** Using water instead of solvents eliminates a major source of chemical emissions.

**Reduced Chemical Complexity:** Waterborne formulations typically require fewer chemical additives than solvent-based products, reducing overall chemical footprint.

**Lower Toxicity:** Water-based finishes are less toxic than solvent-based alternatives, reducing both worker and environmental health risks.

**Easier Remediation:** Water-based products are easier to clean up and dispose of responsibly than solvent-based finishes.

**Renewable Technology:** Water is a renewable resource, whereas many solvents are petroleum-derived and non-renewable.

## Slide 7: Proper Ventilation and Respiratory Protection

### Narrative
Even though ARROYO® 1K has lower VOC emissions, proper ventilation is still recommended.

**Adequate Ventilation:**
- Ensure 8 to 10 air changes per hour in finishing areas
- Use spot ventilation near the application area
- Avoid air movement that would dust-contaminate the finish
- Balance exhaust with make-up air to prevent negative pressure

**Respiratory Protection:**
- Most applications of ARROYO® 1K don't require respiratory protection due to low VOC content
- For sensitive individuals or prolonged exposure, a dust mask may be appropriate
- If using additives or thinners, verify whether they require respiratory protection
- Always follow OSHA guidance for your specific application

The low-VOC nature of ARROYO® 1K means most finishers can work more comfortably than with solvent-based products.

## Slide 9: Responsible Disposal Methods

### Narrative
Proper disposal of ARROYO® 1K and wash water is essential for environmental protection.

**Excess Material:**
- Small quantities of excess ARROYO® 1K can often be allowed to dry in a waste container and disposed of as solid waste
- Check local regulations for specific guidance
- Never pour liquid finish down drains
- Never incinerate liquid finish

**Wash Water:**
- Allow wash water to settle in a suitable container
- Solids will settle to the bottom; clear liquid on top can be disposed of per local regulations
- Or, treat wash water like excess material: allow to fully dry, then dispose as solid waste
- Never dump wash water into storm drains

**Contaminated Materials:**
- Materials contaminated with ARROYO® 1K can often be disposed of as solid waste after drying
- Some facilities have special waste collection programs; check with your waste management provider

**Best Practice:**
- Contact your local environmental agency for specific disposal guidance in your area
- Some areas have hazardous waste collection events for proper disposal
